Frequently Asked Questions

We’ve compiled answers to common questions to help guide you through the legal process with clarity and confidence.

The probate process in Trinidad generally takes between 18 to 24 months. While we work efficiently to manage your case, the timeline is largely influenced by the legal system and procedures in place. We’ll ensure everything on our end is handled promptly to help the process move as smoothly as possible.

Having a lawyer ensures the process is handled correctly and efficiently, helping to avoid any legal complications. In Trinidad and Tobago, if the estate is valued at over $4,800.00, the application for probate must be made through an Attorney-at-Law.

During the first consultation, we’ll discuss your legal needs, review your case, and provide clear guidance on the next steps. Our goal is to ensure you understand your options and feel confident moving forward.

Estate planning occurs during a person’s lifetime and involves creating legal documents like wills and trusts to manage and distribute assets. Probate, on the other hand, is the legal process that takes place after someone passes away to administer their estate according to their will or local laws.
